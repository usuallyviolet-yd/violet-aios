# Reasoning Models

## Simple Definition

Reasoning models are AI models optimized to spend more effort solving complex tasks, especially tasks requiring multiple steps, planning, math, coding, analysis, or careful decision-making.

## Human Analogy

A regular model answers quickly. A reasoning model is more like someone who pauses, thinks through the problem, checks alternatives, and then answers.

## Why Reasoning Matters

Reasoning improves performance on tasks where a shallow answer is risky, such as:

- complex product strategy
- code generation and debugging
- mathematical or logical reasoning
- multi-step workflow planning
- legal/financial/technical analysis
- agent planning and tool use

## Tradeoffs

Reasoning usually increases:

- latency
- token usage
- cost

So product teams should choose reasoning effort based on task value and risk.

## Product Lens

Reasoning is not always necessary. For simple classification or rewriting, fast models may be enough. For high-stakes workflow decisions, deeper reasoning may be worth the cost.

## Violet Connection

Reasoning models are important for AI product management because they change how workflows are designed: some tasks can move from “human analysis first” to “AI drafts analysis, human reviews.”
